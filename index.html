VERTICAL FARMING

A vertical farm grows plants and produce by stacking
the plants. This is accomplished by using growing shelves
suspended on a wall or fence, which uses much less space
than growing the plants on the ground. Thereby,
maximizing the available space.

Hydroponics

Cultivating plants by placing the roots
in liquid nutrient solutions rather
than in soil;
soilless growth of plant.

Aeroponics

The roots of the plants hang suspended
in the air while nutrient solution is
delivered to them in the form of
a fine mist.

Aquaponics

Waste produced by farmed fish or other
aquatic creatures supplies the nutrients for
plants grown hydroponically,which in turn
purify the water

Eco India: The hyperlocal farm delivering freshly-harvested,
leafy greens right to your doorstep

Advantages of vertical farming:

  • Continuous Crop Production
  • Elimination of Herbicides and Pesticides
  • Protection from Weather-Related Variations in Crop Production
  • Water Conservation and Recycling
  • People Friendly
  • More produce in less space
